Enteral Nutritional Support in Critically Ill Patients

Clinical calculator based on ASPEN / ESPEN guidelines

Daily Energy Requirement

Estimation using indirect calorimetry, the Fick method, or predictive equations.

Indirect Calorimetry

EE = [(3.941 × VO₂) + (1.106 × VCO₂)] × 1440

—

Fick Method

VO₂ = CO × (CaO₂ − CvO₂) × 10

— —

Predictive Equations

20, 25 and 30 kcal/kg/day

— — —
Clinical interpretation:
Indirect calorimetry is the preferred method whenever available. The Fick method can estimate VO₂ when cardiac output and arterial/venous blood gas data are available. Predictive equations provide approximate values and should be individualized according to clinical phase, tolerance, and risk of overfeeding.

Important clinical note

• In patients with burns >15% total body surface area (TBSA), protein requirements may be significantly increased, even in the presence of chronic kidney disease.

• In patients with obesity (BMI >30 kg/m²), consider using ideal body weight or adjusted body weight for calorie estimation, while maintaining a hypocaloric / high-protein strategy according to ASPEN / ESPEN guidelines.

• Enteral formula suggestions are for guidance only and do not replace clinical judgment. Consider modular protein supplementation if a protein deficit persists.
